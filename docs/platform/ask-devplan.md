# Ask Devplan

A conversational AI assistant scoped to your workspace. Ask questions about product changes, project status, blockers, and decisions — and get answers grounded in your connected sources and workspace context.

---

## How to access

Click **Ask Devplan** in the left sidebar. Opening the page creates or resumes a chat session automatically.

---

## Key features

- **Chat input** — ask anything in natural language, with support for file attachments
- **Suggested prompts** — starter questions like "What changed in our product this week?" and "What open risks or blockers should I know about?"
- **Evidence pills** — inline source citations in assistant responses (see below)
- **Chat history** — left rail lists recent chats with search
- **Editable titles** — rename chats to keep your history organized

---

## Evidence pills

When Devplan cites connected sources in a response, you'll see inline **evidence pills** — small rounded badges showing stacked source icons (GitHub, Slack, Jira, etc.) and a count like `3 sources`. Pills appear inline within the answer text, next to the claims they support.

Click an evidence pill to open a **Sources** flyout on the right side of the screen. The flyout lists each reference behind that claim:

- **Source icons and rationale** — see which integration produced each reference and why it was cited
- **Origin links** — open the original PR, Slack thread, ticket, or document
- **Details toggle** — expand a row to read captured content, metadata, and linked artifacts

While a response is still streaming, evidence pills may appear after Devplan finishes resolving source links.

---

## Common workflows

1. Click **Ask Devplan** and choose a suggested prompt or type your own question.
2. Attach files or links for additional context when needed.
3. Click an **evidence pill** in a response to inspect the sources behind a claim.
4. Continue the conversation to drill into details.
5. Start a **New chat** from the sidebar when you want a fresh thread.
6. Search past chats to find earlier answers.
